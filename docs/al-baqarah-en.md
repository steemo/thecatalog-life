# Surah Al-Baqarah (The Cow)

**Created by:** Tiko Abousteit  
**Date:** 21 February 2026

---

## Main Objective

Preparing the Ummah for building civilization on earth and establishing Allah's religion, while clarifying the categories of people in this regard.

---

## Surah Overview

### Basic Information
- **Type**: Medinan (revealed after Hijrah)
- **Order**: 2nd Surah in the Quran (after Al-Fatihah)
- **Location**: Starts in Juz 1, continues until Juz 3
- **Length**: Longest Surah in the Quran
- **Verses**: 286 verses
- **Words**: 6,144 words
- **Reward**: ~145,000 good deeds per complete recitation

---

## General Structure (Major Sections)

### 1. Introduction (1-20)
Categories of people (believers, disbelievers, hypocrites)

### 2. Succession on Earth (21-39)
Story of Prophet Adam (peace be upon him)

### 3. Bani Israel (40-123)
Model of failed succession (to learn from their mistakes)

### 4. Prophet Ibrahim (124-141)
Model of successful succession (building the Kaaba and trials)

### 5. Laws for the Successor Nation (142-283)
Qiblah, patience, worship, transactions, and usury

### 6. Conclusion (284-286)
Prayer of submission and seeking refuge in Allah

## Names of the Surah

### Most Common Name: "Surah Al-Baqarah" (The Cow)
- **Reason**: Named after an incident involving Bani Israel, where Allah commanded them through Prophet Musa to slaughter a cow to reveal a murderer.

### Other Names:
1. **Az-Zahra** (The Radiant) - Light and illumination
2. **Sanam Al-Quran** (The Hump of the Quran) - Elevation and nobility
3. **Fustat Al-Quran** (The Tent of the Quran) - Comprehensiveness and encompassment

## Virtues of the Surah

### From Prophetic Traditions:

**1. Expelling Satan:**
> "Do not make your houses like graveyards. Indeed, Satan flees from a house in which Surah Al-Baqarah is recited."

**2. The Two Radiant Ones:**
> "Recite the two radiant ones: Al-Baqarah and Aal-Imran. They will come on the Day of Judgment like two clouds, two shades, or two flocks of birds in ranks, defending their companion."

**3. Greatest Verse:**
Contains the greatest verse in the Quran: Ayat Al-Kursi (verse 255).

## Connections with Other Surahs

### Connection with Surah Al-Fatihah

#### 1. Supplication and Response:
- **Al-Fatihah**: "Guide us to the straight path"

#### 2. Summary and Detail:

#### 3. Succession on Earth:

## Summary of the Surah

### Beginning: Call to Taqwa
The Surah begins with a general call from Allah to all people for God-consciousness (Taqwa).

### Three Categories of People:
1. **Believers** (4 verses)
2. **Disbelievers** (2 verses)
3. **Hypocrites** (13 verses + two parables)

### Story of Adam and Succession:
"Indeed, I will make upon the earth a successive authority"

### Three Models of Succession:

#### First Model: Prophet Adam
- Disobeyed Allah, then repented and turned back
- "Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves"

#### Second Model: Bani Israel
- "We hear and we disobey"
- 15 blessings from Allah
- 10 transgressions they committed
- 10 punishments from Allah

#### Third Model: Prophet Ibrahim
- "We hear and we obey"
- "And when his Lord tested Ibrahim with commands, and he fulfilled them"

### Divine Commands and Laws:
- Prayer, fasting, and Hajj
- Retaliation and wills
- Marriage, divorce, and nursing
- Financial transactions and usury
- Debt and collateral

### Conclusion: Supplication
"Our Lord, do not impose blame upon us if we forget or make a mistake... And give us victory over the disbelieving people"

## Gems of the Surah

### 1. Unity of Origin
"From a single soul" - All humanity created from one origin

### 2. Consoling Hearts
"And speak to them a kind word" - Giving "consolation" to relatives in inheritance matters

### 3. Mercy in Legislation
"Allah wants to lighten for you" - Legislation came for ease, not hardship

### 4. Absolute Justice
"Indeed, Allah does not do injustice, even as much as an atom's weight"

### 5. Better Greeting
"Then greet with better than it" - Excellence in interaction

---

## Key Messages

### 1. Perseverance on the Path
Success is not in beginnings, but in endings.

### 2. Taqwa as Prerequisite
God-consciousness is the condition for benefiting from the Quran.

### 3. Failure from Within
Failure begins internally (discord and disobedience), not from enemy strength.

### 4. Financial Management
Wealth is a blessing from Allah, and its management must align with Allah's methodology.

### 5. Supplication as Worship
Worship through supplication is among the greatest forms of devotion.
