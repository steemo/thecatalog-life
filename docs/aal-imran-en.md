# Surah Aal-Imran (The Family of Imran)

**Created by:** Tiko Abousteit  
**Date:** 21 February 2026

---

## Main Objective

Establishing the Ummah's awareness of how to remain steadfast on the path, based on pure faith and clear identity, while defining their framework for dealing with external forces, so that no trial shakes them and no doubt breaks them.

---

## Surah Overview

### Basic Information
- **Type**: Medinan (revealed after Hijrah)
- **Order**: 3rd Surah in the Quran
- **Location**: Starts in the middle of Juz 3, continues until near the end of Juz 4
- **Length**: Second longest Surah in the Quran after Al-Baqarah
- **Verses**: 200 verses
- **Words**: ~3,480 words
- **Reward**: ~145,000 good deeds per complete recitation

---

## Names of the Surah

### Most Common Name: "Surah Aal-Imran" (The Family of Imran)
- **Reason**: Named after the story of the family of Imran (Imran, father of Maryam) and Prophet Isa and Maryam (peace be upon them).

### Second Name: "The Second Radiant One"
- Together with Surah Al-Baqarah, they are called "The Two Radiant Ones"

---

## Virtues of the Surah

### From Prophetic Traditions:

**The Two Radiant Ones:**
> "Recite the Quran, for it will come on the Day of Judgment as an intercessor for its companions. Recite the two radiant ones: Al-Baqarah and Aal-Imran."

**The Greatest Name of Allah:**
> "A man recited Al-Baqarah and Aal-Imran in the presence of Abdullah, who said: 'You have recited two Surahs containing the Greatest Name of Allah.'"

## Connections with Other Surahs

### Connection with Surah Al-Baqarah

#### 1. Purpose and Objective:
- **Al-Baqarah**: Giving the methodology and establishing laws (What to do?)
- **Aal-Imran**: Protecting the methodology and remaining steadfast (How to stay firm on what you do?)

#### 2. Openings and Conclusions:
- **Al-Baqarah**: Ended with a great supplication "And give us victory over the disbelieving people"
- **Aal-Imran**: Began with the response to this supplication, detailing how victory comes

#### 3. Stories of Succession:
- **Al-Baqarah**: Story of Adam (creation of humanity)
- **Aal-Imran**: Story of the family of Imran, Isa, and Maryam (evolution of humanity)

#### 4. Responding to Opponents:
- **Al-Baqarah**: Focused on the Jews (Bani Israel)
- **Aal-Imran**: Focused on the Christians (Christians of Najran)

## Summary of the Surah

### Beginning: Establishing Faith and Monotheism
"Allah - there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence"

### Debates with the Delegation of Najran (1-83):
- Responding to their doubts about Prophet Isa
- Story of Imran's wife's pregnancy with Maryam
- Allah's glad tidings of his birth without a father
- Miracles performed by Allah through Prophet Isa

### Four Calls to the People of the Scripture:
1. "Say, 'O People of the Scripture, come to a word...'" (64)
2. "O People of the Scripture, why do you argue..." (65)
3. "O People of the Scripture, why do you disbelieve..." (70)
4. "O People of the Scripture, why do you confuse..." (71)

### Call to the Believers (100-120):
- Holding fast to the rope of Allah
- Calling to goodness, enjoining right, and forbidding wrong
- Warning against obeying or taking People of the Scripture as allies

### Battles of Badr and Uhud (121-175):
- Allah's victory for believers at Badr by supporting them with angels
- Reasons for Muslims' defeat at Uhud due to disobeying the Messenger's orders
- Exposing the hypocrites' position

### Conclusion: Establishing Faith and Supplication (176-200):
- "Our Lord, let not our hearts deviate after You have guided us"
- "Our Lord, surely You will gather the people for a Day about which there is no doubt"

### Theme 1 (1-99): Exposing the corruption of the People of the Scripture's beliefs
### Theme 2 (100-120): Calling to hold fast and prepare to face enemies
### Theme 3 (121-200): Two models of confrontations with the Ummah's enemies

## Pillars of Steadfastness

### 1. Firm Knowledge
"And those firm in knowledge"

### 2. Worship of Allah and Understanding Purpose
"O Maryam, be devoutly obedient to your Lord and prostrate and bow with those who bow"

### 3. Remembrance and Reflection
"Who remember Allah while standing or sitting"

### 4. Supplication and Seeking Refuge in Allah
"Our Lord, let not our hearts deviate after You have guided us"

### 5. Enjoining Right and Forbidding Wrong
"You are the best nation produced for mankind"

### 6. Repentance and Seeking Forgiveness
"Indeed, those of you who turned back on the day the two armies met"

### 7. Trust in Allah's Victory and Certainty
"If you remain patient and conscious of Allah, their plot will not harm you at all"

### 8. Holding Fast to Allah
"And whoever holds firmly to Allah has been guided to a straight path"

### 9. God-Consciousness (Taqwa)
"And hasten to forgiveness from your Lord"

### 10. Patience and Perseverance
"O you who have believed, persevere and endure"

## Gems of the Surah

### 1. Reasons for Allah's Love
- "And Allah loves the doers of good" (134, 148)
- "And Allah loves the steadfast" (146)
- "Indeed, Allah loves those who rely upon Him" (159)

### 2. Allah's Mercy and Kindness to Believers
In all stages of their lives and all their circumstances

### 3. Fairness with People of the Scripture
- "Among the People of the Scripture is a community standing in obedience" (113)
- "Indeed, among the People of the Scripture are those who believe in Allah" (199)

---

## Key Messages

### 1. Steadfastness on the Path
In doubts and desires, establish "constants" (clear principles) that are non-negotiable.

### 2. Repentance and Righteous Deeds
Do not accept "procrastination" — every minute is an opportunity.

### 3. Failure from Within
Begins from (discord and disobedience), not from enemy strength.

### 4. True Righteousness
You will not attain righteousness until you give up what you love most for Allah's sake.

### 5. Financial Management
Wealth is a blessing from Allah, and its management must align with Allah's methodology.
