**Date:** 21 February 2026

## Main Objective

Establishing the foundations of justice and mercy in building Muslim society, and protecting the rights of the vulnerable.

---

## Surah Overview

### Basic Information
- **Type**: Medinan (revealed after Hijrah)
- **Order**: 4th Surah in the Quran
- **Location**: Starts at the end of Juz 4, continues through all of Juz 5, and forty verses of Juz 6
- **Length**: Third longest Surah in the Quran
- **Verses**: 176 verses
- **Words**: ~3,745 words
- **Letters**: ~16,030 letters
- **Reward**: Over 160,000 good deeds per complete recitation

---

## General Structure (Major Sections)

### 1. Organizing Family Affairs and Caring for Orphans (1-35)
Rights of women, inheritance, orphans, and justice in homes.
- **Principle**: Justice begins with those closest to you.

### 2. Organizing Society and State (36-126)
Trusts, obedience to authority, migration for Allah's sake, prayer of fear.
- **Principle**: A strong society protects the weak and fulfills trusts.

### 3. Confronting Internal and External Plots (127-162)
Discussion of hypocrites (internal threat) and People of the Scripture (external threat).
- **Principle**: Steadfastness in justice protects you from enemies' plots.

### 4. Conclusion on Inheritance and Monotheism (163-176)
Affirming the final message, concluding with the verse of "Kalalah" to confirm heirs' rights.
- **Principle**: Allah legislates rights, so do not oppress.

## Names of the Surah

### 1. Official Name: "Surah An-Nisa" (The Women)
- **Reason**: Named for the abundance of rulings related to women (inheritance, marriage, marital rights, orphan girls' rights).

### 2. "The Greater Surah of Women"
- **Reason**: To distinguish it from Surah "At-Talaq" (Divorce), sometimes called "The Lesser Surah of Women".
- **Difference**: An-Nisa (Greater) addressed family, society, politics, and state affairs, while At-Talaq (Lesser) focused specifically on divorce and waiting period rulings.

### 3. Surah of "The Vulnerable" (Reflective Title)
- **Reason**: Revealed to bring justice to the vulnerable in society (women, orphans, children).

## Virtues of the Surah

### "Surah of Weeping and Humility"
Among its greatest virtues is what happened with Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (may Allah be pleased with him), when the Prophet ﷺ said to him:
> "Recite to me"

He said: Shall I recite to you when it was revealed to you? He said:
> "Indeed, I love to hear it from someone else"

So Ibn Mas'ud recited from Surah An-Nisa until he reached Allah's saying:
"So how will it be when We bring from every nation a witness and we bring you, [O Muhammad], against these as a witness?"

The Prophet ﷺ wept until his eyes shed tears.

## Connections with Other Surahs

### Connection with Surah Aal-Imran

#### 1. "War and Consequences" (Battle of Uhud):
- **Aal-Imran**: Detailed the events of the Battle of Uhud and the resulting martyrdom of many Muslims.
- **An-Nisa**: Came to address the "social aftermath" of that war; orphans and widows increased.

#### 2. "Gradation of Calls to Taqwa":
- **Aal-Imran (conclusion)**: "O you who have believed, persevere and endure and remain stationed and fear Allah"
- **An-Nisa (opening)**: "O mankind, fear your Lord, who created you from one soul"

#### 3. "Relationship with People of the Scripture":
- **Aal-Imran**: Focus on debating People of the Scripture about creed and religious fundamentals.
- **An-Nisa**: Discussion shifted to exposing their social plots and betrayals.

## Summary of the Surah

### Beginning: Call to Taqwa
"O mankind, fear your Lord, who created you from one soul"

### Divine Directives:
1. **Orphans** (1)
2. **Family ties** (1)
3. **Permissibility of polygamy** with condition of justice (3)
4. **Woman's right to her dowry** (4)
5. **Dealing with orphans' wealth** (5)
6. **Prohibition of extravagance with orphans' wealth** (6)
7. **Distribution of inheritance** according to Allah's law (7-8)

### Organizing Inheritance (11-14):
- **Children's inheritance**: Male receives twice the female's share
- **Parents' inheritance**: One-sixth each if there are children
- **Spouses' inheritance**: Half or quarter for husband, quarter or eighth for wife
- **Kalalah inheritance**: One who has neither parent nor child

### Prohibited Women for Marriage (23):
- **7 prohibited by blood**: Mother, daughter, sister, paternal aunt, maternal aunt, brother's daughter, sister's daughter
- **7 prohibited by cause**: Foster mother, foster sister, mother-in-law, stepdaughter, father's wife, son's wife, combining two sisters

### Commands and Rulings:
- Rendering trusts to their owners (58)
- Obeying Allah, the Messenger, and those in authority (59)
- Judging by what the Messenger brought (65)
- Going forth for Allah's cause (71)
- Responding and obeying the Messenger (80)
- Reflecting on Allah's verses (82)
- Accidental killing and its ruling (92)
- Intentional killing and its ruling (93)
- Prayer of fear (101-102)

### Conclusion:
"O mankind, there has come to you a conclusive proof from your Lord" (174)

## Gems of the Surah

### 1. "Unity of Origin"
"From one soul" - All humanity created from one origin

### 2. "Consoling Hearts"
"And speak to them a kind word" - Giving "consolation" to relatives in inheritance

### 3. "Mercy in Legislation"
"Allah wants to lighten for you"

### 4. "Resolving Conflict"
"If they both desire reconciliation" - In marital problems

### 5. "Absolute Justice"
"Indeed, Allah does not do injustice, even as much as an atom's weight"

### 6. "Women and Jihad"
"And the oppressed among men, women, and children"

### 7. "Better Greeting"
"Then greet with better than it"

### 8. "Precision in Testimony"
"Even if it be against yourselves"

### 9. "Protecting Hearing and Heart"
"Then do not sit with them"

### 10. "Legal Conclusion"
"They ask you for a legal ruling. Say, 'Allah gives you a ruling'" - Verse of Kalalah

---

## Key Messages

### 1. Justice as Foundation of Society
Determine your position among the three categories: procrastination, debate and objection, or we hear and obey?

### 2. Commands Test Faith
Divine commands test faith and sincerity of obedience.

### 3. Sanctity of Rights
The sanctity of rights related to creation.

### 4. Financial Management
Wealth is a blessing from Allah, and its management must align with Allah's methodology.

### 5. Supplication as Worship
Worship through supplication is among the greatest forms of devotion.
